Madhuri Nagare

Hi! I am a fifth year Ph.D. student in the Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering at Purdue University. I work with Prof. Charles A. Bouman to improve the quality of medical CT (X-ray computed tomography) images. We work at the crossroads of computational imaging, machine learning, and signal processing. Specifically, my research focuses on controlling noise-resolution-texture tradeoff in CT images with deep neural network (DNN) based methods.

Before starting my Ph.D., I spent three amazing years at NEC Corporation, Japan as an industrial researcher. I along with my team developed an atmospheric correction module for satellite images. I graduated with Masters in Geoinformatics from Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay, India in 2015. For my Master's thesis, I worked with Prof. Krishna Mohan Buddhiraju on decision tree classifiers for satellite images. I completed Bachelors in Electronics and Telecommunication from College of Engineering, Pune, India in 2013.

I spend my spare time in gardening and running. Recently, I have been doing powerlifting too!
